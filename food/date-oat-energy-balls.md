# Date-Oat Energy Balls

*Whole-food endurance fuel made with fast-acting dates and sustained-energy oats. Easy to make and carry.*

---

## Ingredients

*Makes 12 balls*

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Medjool dates, pitted | 16 |
| Rolled oats (old-fashioned) | 1 cup |
| Almond butter | 3 tbsp |
| Honey | 2 tbsp |
| Kosher salt | ¾ tsp |
| Dark chocolate chips (70%+) | ¼ cup |
| Puffed rice cereal *(opt., lighter texture)* | ¼ cup |
| Vanilla extract | ½ tsp |
| Finely ground coffee (opt.) | 1 tsp |

---

## Instructions

**Step 1 — Soak dates** *(~10 min)*
If dates feel firm or dry, soak them in warm water for 10 minutes, then drain well. Dates should be soft and sticky — this is key for binding the balls together.

**Step 2 — Pulse dates**
Pulse drained dates in food processor for 20–30 seconds until a sticky paste forms - should look like a rough dough. Don't over-process into a liquid.

**Step 3 — Add wet ingredients & seasoning**
Add nut butter, honey, vanilla extract, kosher salt, and ground coffee. Pulse a few more times until everything is combined and mixture uniform.

**Step 4 — Fold in dry ingredients**
Transfer mixture to large bowl. Add rolled oats (and puffed rice cereal, if using), fold in by hand using a spatula or your hands until evenly distributed. Then fold in the chocolate chips.

**Step 5 — Chill mixture** *(20–30 min)*
Cover bowl and refrigerate for 20–30 minutes - this firms up the mixture, easier to roll into balls.

**Step 6 — Roll into balls**
With slightly damp hands (prevents sticking), scoop about 2 tablespoons of mixture and roll firmly between your palms into a ball. Aim for roughly golf-ball size. Place on a parchment-lined tray.

**Step 7 — Final chill & store** *(30 min)*
Refrigerate the finished balls for at least 30 minutes to set. Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 weeks, or freeze for up to 3 months. For runs, wrap individually in parchment paper squares.

---

## Endurance fueling notes

- **Salt:** ~200mg sodium per ball. Using Diamond Crystal (coarser).
- **Caffeine:** 1 tsp finely ground coffee = ~20–30mg caffeine per ball. Increase to 1½ tsp for a stronger kick.
- **Carb boost:** Replace ¼ cup oats with puffed white rice for faster digestion mid-run.
- **Nut-free version:** Swap nut butter for sunflower seed butter (SunButter).
- **On the run:** Each ball = ~150–170 calories. 1 ball every 30–45 minutes on long efforts.
- **Caffeine strategy:** Consider making two batches — one plain, one caffeinated — so you can control your caffeine timing on long runs (plain balls early, caffeinated in the final third).
